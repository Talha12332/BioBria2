Lecture-1 : Mitochondria and it's structure

Mitochondria are membrane-bound organelles found in the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells. Often referred to as the "powerhouses" of the cell, their primary function is to produce adenosine triphosphate (ATP), the cell's main energy source, through a process called cellular respiration. Mitochondria have their own DNA and ribosomes, allowing them to replicate independently of the cell. They also play a role in regulating cellular metabolism, calcium balance, and apoptosis (programmed cell death). Due to their unique features, mitochondria are thought to have originated from ancient symbiotic bacteria.
